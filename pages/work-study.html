Work & Study in Germany

Germany is an attractive destination for international talent. With tuition-free universities and new visa pathways like the Opportunity Card, building a future here has never been more accessible.

Higher Education

Germany is renowned for its high-quality education system. Most public universities are tuition-free, even for international students.

Why Study in Germany?

  • No Tuition Fees: Public universities in 15 of the 16 states charge no tuition fees (only a semester contribution of approx. 250€-350€).
  • English Programs: Over 2,000 degree programs are taught entirely in English.
  • Post-Study Work: Graduates can stay for up to 18 months to look for a job relevant to their degree.

Top Universities for Sustainability

  • Technical University of Munich (TUM): Leader in engineering and environmental sciences.
  • University of Freiburg: Located in Germany's "Green City," focusing on renewable energy.
  • Humboldt University Berlin: Strong research focus on climate and social sciences.

The Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)

Introduced to attract skilled workers from outside the EU, the "Chancenkarte" is a points-based job seeker visa.

How It Works

The card allows you to come to Germany for up to one year to look for employment. During this time, you can work part-time (up to 20 hours/week) or do trial work.

Requirements

To qualify, you generally need:

  • Basic Requirements: A recognized vocational or university degree (or a degree recognized in the country of training) and basic German (A1) or English (B2) skills.
  • Points System: You need at least 6 points based on criteria like:
    • Language skills (German/English)
    • Professional experience
    • Age (younger applicants get more points)
    • Connection to Germany (previous stays)

Language Skills

While many Germans speak English, learning German is crucial for daily life and many jobs. From A1 basics to C2 fluency, there are many ways to learn.
